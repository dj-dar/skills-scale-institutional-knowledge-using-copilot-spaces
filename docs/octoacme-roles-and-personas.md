# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Agile Coach / Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Agile Coaches and Scrum Masters guide teams in agile practices, facilitate ceremonies, and help remove blockers to maintain healthy delivery cadence. They act as servant-leaders who coach rather than direct.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies: standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove process impediments that slow delivery
- Coach team members and Project Manager on agile best practices
- Support continuous improvement by tracking and acting on retrospective outcomes
- Mentor team on backlog refinement and acceptance criteria quality

### Goals
- Build a high-performing, self-organizing team
- Reduce waste and increase predictable delivery
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup facilitation and blocker triage
- Retrospective facilitation and action-item follow-up
- Agile health checks and team coaching sessions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works closely with **Project Manager** to align delivery planning and remove cross-team blockers
- Coaches **Developers** and **QA** on process best practices and sprint hygiene
- Supports **Product Manager** in effective backlog refinement and story sizing

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape how users experience the product. They bridge customer needs and technical implementation by creating intuitive user flows, interfaces, and validated design artifacts.

### Responsibilities
- Design user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups
- Conduct user research and usability testing to validate designs
- Collaborate with Product Manager to translate requirements into design specifications
- Define and refine acceptance criteria related to usability and user experience
- Ensure design consistency with brand guidelines and accessibility standards

### Goals
- Deliver experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs
- Reduce rework by validating designs early with real users
- Bridge the gap between business requirements and technical implementation

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions with Product Manager and Developers
- Usability test results and research summaries shared with Stakeholders
- Annotated mockups and design specs handed off to Developers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Collaborates with **Product Manager** to translate requirements into user-centered designs
- Partners with **Developers** to ensure design fidelity during implementation
- Engages **Stakeholders** to gather feedback and validate designs against business goals

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide deep domain or technical knowledge — such as compliance, security, data architecture, or industry regulations — to ensure plans and user stories are feasible, accurate, and risk-aware.

### Responsibilities
- Review project plans, user stories, and technical designs for feasibility and domain risk
- Provide authoritative guidance on regulatory, compliance, or industry best practices
- Identify and communicate constraints or requirements from their area of expertise
- Validate that deliverables meet domain-specific standards before release
- Serve as a point of escalation for domain-specific questions

### Goals
- Ensure the project meets regulatory, security, or domain standards
- Reduce risk by surfacing knowledge gaps and constraints early
- Improve solution quality through expert input at key design and review points

### Typical Communication
- Consultations with Project Manager, Product Manager, and Developers at design and review milestones
- Written sign-offs or approval documentation for compliance checkpoints
- Ad-hoc expert reviews for high-risk or complex features

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Consults with **Project Manager** to flag constraints that affect timelines or scope
- Advises **Product Manager** on feasibility and regulatory implications of requirements
- Reviews **Developer** designs and implementations for domain compliance

---

## Support / Release Coordinator

### Role Summary
Support and Release Coordinators ensure smooth handoffs between development and production. They coordinate release timing, run post-release validation, and act as the bridge between the delivery team and support operations.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate release scheduling and deployment windows with engineering and operations teams
- Run post-release smoke tests and validation checks
- Prepare support teams with release notes, known issues, and escalation paths
- Collect and aggregate user feedback and incident reports during rollout
- Manage rollback procedures if critical issues are detected post-release

### Goals
- Ensure releases go live smoothly with minimal customer disruption
- Maintain clear support readiness so users get timely help after each release
- Close the feedback loop between production incidents and future planning

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness checklists and go/no-go meetings with Project Manager and Developers
- Post-release status updates and incident summaries to Stakeholders
- Handoff documentation and runbooks shared with the support team

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works with **Project Manager** during release planning to align timing and risk mitigation
- Partners with **Developers** to confirm technical readiness and prepare rollback plans
- Briefs **Stakeholders** on release timelines, known risks, and support preparedness
